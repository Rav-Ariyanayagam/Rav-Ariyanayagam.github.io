3. Reflecting on problem-solving techniques and processes

Trying something, Googling, Asking coaches and peers for help, Reflection

I'm already very familiar with all of these techniques and regularly employ them as part of my ongoing learning and development. As my preference will always be to able to solve a problem myself, I can be somewhat reluctant to (quickly) ask for help from a peer or coach but, that said, I am also not afraid to admit when I have reached the limits of trying something or Googling for help, and to seek assistance from someone else, be it a peer or a coach.

Pseudocode, Console.logging, Rubber Ducky method, and Reading error messages

Pseudocode is something that I have already employed without actually knowing that there was term for it! I've always found it useful to try to articulate things in different ways and to try to break down a complex problem into smaller pieces to make it more manageable and less intimidating, and the Rubber Ducky method can be very helpful for that. I still don't use console.logging as much as I probably could and it's a diagnostic tool that I've realised I really should be using far more often to help provide me with useful information. Finally, I read the error messages and do take note of the information in there, and when I can understand them and what I have to do to fix the error, it's another very useful tool.
